♥ The Game in One Breath

You pilot a tiny cursor character through a haunted Y2K internet — broken websites, popup windows, error dialogs everywhere. Your "weapons" are browser tabs open in a strip across the top of your screen. They fire themselves on their own rhythm. You don't shoot the tabs — you arrange them, combine them, and try to keep them all happy while greedier and greedier builds pile up.

Every tab costs RAM. You only have so much. Go over your budget and the system starts running hot — eventually malware tabs force themselves into your strip as punishment. That's the gamble: how greedy can you be before the system bites back?

Think Balatro's combo discovery + Vampire Survivors' "watch your build work" energy + Slay the Spire's map progression + the chaotic feeling of having too many Internet Explorer windows open at 2am in 2003.

♥ Our Design Philosophy

"The core feeling we are chasing is SURPRISE, not SCALE."

There are two ways a build can feel powerful:

  • SCALE — everything gets huge and fast. Numbers go up. (Vampire Survivors at hour two.)
  • SURPRISE — effects interact in a way nobody designed. A chain reaction the developer didn't author. That feeling of "...wait, why is that happening?"

We want surprise. Always surprise. If a feature only makes the numbers bigger, it's low priority. If it creates a new interaction path between things — that's what we build.

How we get there

We never write code that says "if this tab AND that tab, then [special thing]." That kind of thinking is a maintenance nightmare and it kills emergence. Instead, every tab is written completely independently. They only touch shared values and shared moments. Interactions emerge because two tabs happen to read the same data — not because we wired them together.

Twenty independently-written tabs should produce hundreds of unauthored interaction paths. The player should discover combos that surprise the developer.

♥ The Five Things on Your Strip

Everything in the game falls into one of five categories. Three of them are tabs (they go in your strip), and two of them are components (crafting materials that go in your inventory).

► ACTIVE

The "weapons." They have a trigger (every 2 seconds, when you take damage, every kill, etc.) and they DO something when triggered (fire a projectile, throw up a shield, swap tabs around).

► SUPPORT

Sit quietly in your strip and buff OTHER tabs based on rules. "+20% damage to TECH tabs" or "the last tab in your strip has +50% damage." They don't fire on their own.

► MALWARE

The bad guys. They force themselves into your strip when you're running too hot. They take up RAM AND debuff you. You have to pay to remove them at shops.

► MODIFIER

Crafting components. Numeric upgrades like "+13% damage" or "+10% fire rate." Combine them with a tab at a shop to bake the buff INTO that specific tab forever.

► EFFECT / EXTENSION

Crafting components. Add NEW behaviors to tabs — Pierce (projectiles go through enemies), Lifesteal (damage heals you), Homing (projectiles seek targets). Compile them onto a tab.

★ ACTIVE TABS — the autofire weapons

The "doing things" tabs. They have a trigger and they perform an action when triggered. The action can be anything — firing a projectile, generating a shield, healing you, even messing with other tabs in your strip.

The trigger

Most ACTIVE tabs trigger every N seconds (ON_TICK), like Vampire Survivors weapons. But triggers can be anything:

  • ON_TICK — every 2 seconds (the classic)
  • ON_KILL — fires when an enemy dies
  • ON_HIT — fires when something you do connects
  • ON_DAMAGE_TAKEN — fires when you get hurt
  • ON_TAB_FIRED — fires when ANOTHER tab fires (chain combos!)
  • ON_DASH — fires when you dash
  • ON_RAM_OVER — fires when you're over RAM budget
  • ON_HEAT_CHANGE — fires when heat crosses a threshold

Example ACTIVE tabs we might build

📄 homepage.html

Fires a small projectile toward the nearest enemy every 1 second. The starter tab. Reliable, boring, perfect.

💥 HEAVY_SHOT.exe

Charges up over 3 seconds, then releases a big slow projectile that pierces multiple enemies. High damage, slow rhythm.

⛓ chain-reaction.exe

Hits the nearest enemy with lightning, which arcs to 3 more nearby enemies. Each chain weaker than the last.

🛡 FIREWALL.dll

Pulses a defensive shield around you every 4 seconds. Absorbs one hit before fading. Defensive ACTIVE.

👻 Volatile.tmp

Every 10 seconds, swaps positions with a random other tab in your strip. Chaotic! Position-aware builds hate this.

💀 Vampire.exe

When opened, closes the tab to its left and gains its base damage. A predator tab. Use it strategically.

The key insight: strip-mutating tabs are still ACTIVE because they have a trigger and they perform an action — the action just happens to change the strip itself instead of shooting at enemies. Same category, different output.

★ SUPPORT TABS — the buffers

SUPPORT tabs sit in your strip and provide passive while-present buffs to specific other tabs. They don't fire on their own. They don't have a trigger. They just exist and shape your build.

Their power is targeting. Instead of "everyone gets +20% damage" they say "only TECH tabs get +20% damage" or "only the tab to my right gets +50% fire rate" or "only the last tab in the strip gains pierce."

This is where build identity gets interesting — SUPPORT tabs make WHICH tabs you bring matter, not just HOW MANY.

Example SUPPORT tabs

⚡ TECH_AMP.dll

All TECH-affinity tabs in your strip gain +20% damage while this is present. Pure damage boost for a specific build path.

🔗 Catalyst.so

The tabs directly adjacent to this one fire 25% faster. Position-aware — reordering changes who benefits.

⚓ Anchor.cfg

The LAST tab in your strip gains +50% damage. Put your nuke on the end.

🏗 Loadbearing.lib

The FIRST tab in your strip costs -1 RAM. Mostly RAM relief, lets you fit a more expensive opener.

📢 Resonator.tmp

Same-affinity adjacent tabs amplify each other. Reward tight color-coded clusters in your strip.

♾ Modifier_Synergy

Each compiled-modifier on any tab grants +5% damage to ALL tabs. Rewards a heavily crafted build.

SUPPORT is where strip position becomes a real build axis. Two players with the same tabs in different orders can have wildly different builds.

★ MALWARE — the consequences

When you've been running over your RAM budget for too long, the system retaliates by forcing MALWARE tabs into your strip. They're bad. They take up RAM (yes, the thing causing the problem) AND they debuff you.

The four tiers

⚠ NUISANCE

Mild. -2.5% damage, -2.5% fire rate, +2.5% cooldowns. Annoying but survivable. Often you just play through it.

⚠⚠ DISRUPTIVE

Real. -5% damage, -5% fire rate, +5% cooldowns. You feel this one. Definitely worth purging at the next shop.

⚠⚠⚠ CRITICAL

-8.75%/-8.75%/+8.75%. Your build is hurting. Drop everything and get to a shop.

☠ FATAL

-12.5%/-12.5%/+12.5%. End of the world numbers. You let it get really bad. Probably a run-ender.

How malware spawns

Heat is measured 0-100. When you complete a zone, the system rolls. Higher heat = higher chance of malware AND higher chance of worse tiers.

SAFE 0-9%
LOW 10-24%
MED 25-44%
HIGH 45-64%
CRITICAL 65-84%
EXTREME 85-100%

At SAFE you're fine. At EXTREME, malware spawn chance is 90%, and the tiers are bad. Skilled players know how many zones they can greed before pulling back to cool down.

The malware system punishes greed but rewards mastery. New players learn the window the hard way. Veterans dance right on the line, in and out of "critical heat" deliberately.

♥ Components — Crafting Materials

Components are not tabs. They're crafting materials. They live in a separate inventory section (we'll show this in a moment). They never appear in your tab strip in their raw form. Their effects only show up after you compile them onto a tab at a shop.

There are two kinds:

MODIFIER components — stat upgrades

These are numbers. "+13% damage" or "+10% fire rate" or "+1 projectile." When you compile a MODIFIER into a tab, that tab gains that stat boost forever. The modifier is consumed.

EFFECT (Extension) components — new behaviors

These add abilities the tab didn't have. Pierce makes projectiles pass through enemies. Lifesteal makes damage heal you. Homing makes projectiles seek targets. Chain makes hits arc to nearby enemies.

Example components

🔧 +13% Damage

Common MODIFIER. Compile onto any damage-dealing tab to bake in extra damage.

⚡ +10% Fire Rate

Common MODIFIER. Compile onto any cadence-based tab to make it fire faster.

♨ TECH Conversion

Rare MODIFIER. Adds TECH affinity to a tab. Now your Homepage is a TECH tab — unlocks new SUPPORT synergies.

🎯 Pierce

EFFECT. Projectiles pass through enemies, dealing damage to each. Requires a tab that fires projectiles.

🩸 Lifesteal

EFFECT. Damage dealt heals you. Works on anything that damages enemies — even a thorny shield.

🔥 Burn

EFFECT. Hits apply burn — damage over time. Pairs with anything that says "+damage to BURNing enemies."

📍 Homing

EFFECT. Projectiles seek the nearest enemy. Massive accuracy boost. Great on slow tabs.

↩ Ricochet

EFFECT. Projectiles bounce off walls and enemies up to 3 times. Chaotic but powerful in tight spaces.

Components solve a real design problem: they let your tabs grow over a run. Your starting Homepage isn't the same Homepage at hour 1. It's gained Pierce, +30% damage, Homing, and TECH affinity. It's yours.

The two sections

  • Top (4 slots) — Tab Storage: Holds ACTIVE and SUPPORT tabs you've picked up but aren't currently using in your strip. Free to store, no RAM cost. Swap tabs in and out as your build changes.
  • Bottom (6 slots) — Program Extensions: Holds your MODIFIER and EFFECT components. These are crafting materials waiting to be compiled onto a tab at a shop. Also free to store.

Both section sizes can be upgraded — through meta progression unlocks, shop purchases, augment picks, event rewards, or artifacts that grant extra slots.

MALWARE never enters your inventory. You can't put it there to "save it for later." It exists only in your strip and only the shop can remove it (for currency).

Compatibility (the smart part)

Not every component fits every tab. Each tab declares what it IS through "host tags" — things like projectile, shield, damages_enemies, pulse. Components declare what they need.

  • Pierce requires a tab tagged projectile. You can't put Pierce on a healing aura.
  • Lifesteal requires damages_enemies. Works on projectile tabs, AND on a defensive shield with thorns.
  • Fire Rate +10% requires pulse (anything with a cadence). Works on projectile tabs AND pulsing shields.
  • Thorns requires shield. And when you add it, the shield GAINS the damages_enemies tag — now you can compile Lifesteal onto it too.
This is where the chains get interesting. Adding Thorns to a defensive shield unlocks new compile possibilities. The build paths multiply as you craft. That is emergence.

Three tabs that each gave +13% damage become ONE tab that gives +43.7% damage (the sum: 39%, plus a 12% bonus for merging). And critically, you've gone from 3 RAM slots used to 1 RAM slot used. Two slots freed up.

Why merge isn't just "+3x"

This part matters: in earlier designs, merge multiplied everything by 3x. That broke the math because merged tabs were saving RAM AND multiplying damage — too generous, no decision tension.

Now merge gives you the sum of what you had, plus a small bonus (~12%). You're saving RAM, you're getting a small power bump for the effort. The choice is meaningful: do I have the currency to merge, or do I need that money for something else?

Both COMPILE and MERGE happen at shops (or forges, or special NPCs) and both cost currency. They're the two crafting decisions you make between fights.

♥ How Tabs Talk to Each Other

This is the deepest part of the design — and it's why we can write 20 tabs and get hundreds of combos.

Tabs never reference each other by name. They never say "if homepage is in the strip then do X." Instead, they all subscribe to shared events and read/write shared values.

An example chain reaction

1
homepage.html fires a projectile at the nearest enemy (ON_TICK every 1 second). The projectile hits an enemy.
2
An ON_HIT event broadcasts globally. Any tab listening for ON_HIT activates.
3
Burn.dll heard the ON_HIT. It applies BURN to the enemy. The enemy starts taking damage over time.
4
The burn damage eventually kills the enemy. An ON_KILL event broadcasts.
5
Detonator.exe heard the ON_KILL. It causes the dead enemy to explode, damaging nearby enemies.
6
Those explosion hits broadcast more ON_HIT events. Burn.dll applies BURN to all of them. Now MORE enemies are burning.
7
The burning enemies start dying. More ON_KILL events. More explosions. More ON_HIT events. The chain feeds itself.

None of those tabs were designed to work together. Burn.dll doesn't know Detonator exists. Detonator doesn't know Burn exists. Homepage doesn't know either of them. But because they all subscribe to the same shared events, their combination produces a chain reaction nobody authored.

That moment when the player discovers this? That's the magic. "...wait. WAIT. Why is everything exploding?"

The depth cap exists so chains can't run forever (max 8 levels deep, then it stops). But within those 8 levels you can produce screen-clearing payoffs from very simple parts. This is the headline feeling of the entire game.

♥ The Five Affinities

Affinities are build identity colors. Each tab can have a primary affinity (and sometimes a secondary). Affinities don't do anything by themselves — they're labels. But they unlock build paths through augments and SUPPORT tabs.

"All TECH tabs gain +20% damage" doesn't help if you have no TECH tabs. But if you've been collecting TECH tabs all run, that augment becomes huge.

TECH
Efficiency. RAM management. "While RAM under ceiling" conditions. Cool, calculated, surgical. Color: cyan / terminal green.
OCCULT
Kill scaling. Momentum. "Per kill" stacking. The longer the run goes, the stronger you get. Color: deep purple / blood red.
OVERCLOCKED
Punishment-as-power. Heat and malware GIVE you buffs instead of just hurting you. Be over budget. Take the malware. Get stronger. Color: orange / fire.
GLITCH
Chance. Variance. RNG payouts. Random crits, skip enemy defense, lottery wins. Embrace the chaos. Color: corrupted green.
FIREWALL
Sustain. Defense. Damage reduction, shields, healing. The "I will not die" build. Color: cool blue / ice.

The unlock journey

You start with TECH and OCCULT available. The other three unlock through play — completing certain runs, reaching certain depths, etc. Meta progression. Each unlock opens new build archetypes that weren't possible before.

Affinities don't self-stack. Having 5 TECH tabs doesn't automatically buff you. But picking the right SUPPORT tabs or augments for a TECH-heavy strip can transform it. Affinities are opt-in identity.

♥ Active Abilities — Your Two Buttons

Before each run, you select two active abilities. These are the skills you control directly — LMB and RMB. While your tabs auto-fire, your abilities are YOUR action.

Abilities are the skill layer. Tabs are the build layer. They exist simultaneously.

⚡
DASH
Short fast burst movement in your current direction. ~1 second cooldown. Brief invincibility while dashing. Your main escape and repositioning tool.
💥
PULSE BOMB
Throw an explosive that detonates in a wide radius. High damage AOE. ~3 second cooldown. Crowd control and area denial.

Current available abilities

⚡ DASH

The default. Quick dodge in current direction. I-frames during the dash. Repositioning and survival.

💥 PULSE BOMB

AOE explosion. Wide radius, big damage. Slower cooldown. The "clear the room" button.

⚫ BLACK HOLE

Drop a black hole that pulls enemies toward it for 3 seconds. Crowd control king. Great for stacking enemies into your AOE.

Future ability ideas

🛡 EMERGENCY FIREWALL

Brief invulnerability bubble. Long cooldown. Panic button.

🌀 PORTAL

Drop two portals — teleport between them. Movement and positioning ability.

🔄 SYNC

Force all tabs to fire on next ON_TICK. Massive burst combo opener.

⏸ FORCE QUIT

Briefly freeze all enemies. Crowd control. Set up a perfect combo.

📥 DOWNLOAD

Channel for 2 seconds to absorb an enemy projectile, then fire it back amplified. Risk/reward.

♻ DEFRAG

Reset all ability cooldowns AND clear heat. Massive utility, very long cooldown itself.

Abilities are unlocked through meta progression too — you start with Dash plus one other, more unlock as you play. Each combination changes how runs feel.

♥ Artifacts — Your System Tray

Artifacts are passive run buffs. They're equipped before the run starts (3 slots) and they live in the bottom-right system tray as little icons — just like background programs running on an old Windows computer.

They don't fire. They don't take up RAM. They just are, modifying your run silently. They're separate from tabs — different system, different power.

♥ RAM and Heat — The Greed Engine

RAM is a budget. Every tab in your strip costs RAM. You start with maybe 6 RAM. You CAN exceed your RAM ceiling deliberately — that's the greed mechanic.

The greed loop

1
You find a powerful tab that costs 3 RAM. You only have 1 RAM left in your budget. Take it anyway.
2
You're now 2 RAM over budget. After every zone you complete, HEAT rises.
3
The longer you stay over budget, the FASTER heat rises. Multiple zones over = exponentially worse.
4
At higher heat bands, malware starts appearing in your strip. It debuffs you AND takes up RAM (making the problem worse!).
5
You have to choose: drop expensive tabs to get back under budget, OR push to a shop to purge the malware (costs currency).
6
Either way: expert players know how many zones they can greed before the system bites back. That window IS the skill.
RAM upgrades cost currency. So does purging malware. So does compiling, merging, buying new tabs, rerolling shop offers. Currency is constantly scarce — every shop visit is a real decision about what to spend on.

♥ Run Structure — The Map

Every run is divided into 3 stages. Each stage has 5-7 zones, ending in a boss fight. So one run = roughly 18-20 zones, ~45-60 minutes of play.

You don't see the whole map at once. After each zone, you see your next 3-5 choices. You pick one, complete it, then see the NEXT 3-5 choices. The map grows behind you as you play — by the end of a stage it sprawls visibly, showing the path you took and the paths you didn't.

In-round encounters

Even normal COMBAT zones can have surprises. Loot Goblins sometimes spawn — fleeing enemies that drop tons of currency if you catch them. Corrupted Kiosks appear as interactables in the world — mini shops mid-zone. Field Forges let you compile right there in the middle of a fight if you find one.

The map fills with chaos as you explore. By the time you reach stage 3, you can look back and see your entire run laid out as a sprawling, branching path through the corrupted internet. Your run is a story you can see.

♥ Meta Progression — Why You Keep Playing

This is the answer to "why play again?" Every run earns cached_data — a persistent currency that survives between runs. You spend cached_data to unlock things in a tree.

What unlocks

📑 More tabs

You start with 5-7 tabs in the offering pool. Unlock 15-20 more. Each unlock changes what's possible in a run.

🎨 More affinities

You start with TECH and OCCULT. Unlock OVERCLOCKED, GLITCH, FIREWALL through play. Each opens new build paths.

🔧 More components

Basic pool from start. Affinity-specific components unlock by completing runs with that affinity. Cursed components through challenges.

⚡ More artifacts

3 baseline artifacts. Unlock 10-15 more. Each adds a new run-shaping option.

🗺 More zones

Base 3 stages. Unlock "Deep Web" (stage 4), "Dark Web" (stage 5 with unique enemies), and "Lost Internet" (endless mode).

🎭 Cosmetic unlocks

Cursor variants, boot screen variations, title screen themes. Personalize the corrupted internet.

The unlock journey IS the game. Your 50th run feels different from your 5th not because the difficulty changed, but because you have more options. New tabs, new affinities, new components mean new builds, new combinations, new "...wait, why is that happening?" moments.

The point isn't to beat the game once. The point is to keep finding new ways the system can surprise you.
